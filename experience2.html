Electroceramics Research Group

Fall 2021-Spring 2022

In inorganic chemistry, we make use of the transition metals shown on the periodic table. When it comes to synthesis using these metals, it is pertinent to have the theory behind the bonding that comes in consideration for metals. Most transition metals have several oxidation states and depending on these oxidation states, affects the number of valence electrons, thus affecting their low-high spin complex when they bond. It is the arrangement of electrons and their differing energy level in metals that rises to certain properties like ferromagnetism and antiferromagnetism and color. It is important to know that each metal has its own unique properties, from conductivity, toxicity, and oxidation sensitivity that gives these synthesized metals their own characterisics. We aim to utilize the transition metals and their unique properties to design material that best incorporates them into real-world applications.

Equipment used and trained: Ceramic synthesis (batch, mill, sintering and calcine) X-ray Diffraction, Low-temperature Dielectric, Polarization vs Electric Field Measurements, Impedance and Scanning Electron Microscope
