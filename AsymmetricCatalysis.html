Asymmetric Catalysis using Homochiral Reticular Metal-Organic Frameworks

Summer 2021-Current









The need for enantiopure materials and better methods to obtain pure material is growing the discovery of new drugs is rapidly expanding. Current methods use tedious separation methods for enantioseparation once a racmic product is received, but with the recent science or asymmetric organocatalysis, an enantiopure product may be obtain during synthesis. The key to this is using a catalyst with asymmetric or chiral centers. Metal-organic frameworks can be intuitively design using chiral organic linkers to exhibit chiral reconition sites which can bind to chiral subtrates that reflect same polarized light (R or S). The product that may be collected in the solution is thus the opposite configuration. Here I intend to use the same homorchiral isoreticular MOFs used in my drug separation project to see how 1) they serve as catalyst to efficiently produce a product and 2) their enantioselectivities and catalytic activity are related to the pore aperatues of these reticular structures.
