What is Supplemental Instructions?

Supplemental Instructions is an internationally recognized academic support program. It is a non-traditional form of tutoring that focuses on collarboration of historically challenging courses. It is a peer-led study group that focuses on understanding key course concepts in a supportive, interactive learning environment.

I am an SI leader for General Chemistry. I have experience teaching this course as a General Chemistry Teaching Assistant and tutor. I also have experience working with small groups, from leading labs and review sessions, to being a mentor for dozens of students in the dorms as a Resident Assistant. I have experience in pedagogy techniques with students as a prior Learning Assistant in Calculus.

Weekly Survey

SI Tables meet every week for fifty minutes. During this time, there will be a structured plan for the day devised by the SI leader. These tables are active, and the structure or activities often fluctute week-by-week to better fit the needs of each individual group.

I will be the SI Leader supporting General Chemistry this year. During each term, it is expected that I host 5 different study tables, each with about 10 students. Keep in mind that with over 1300 students taking general chemistry each year, spots are competitive. If you would like to register or learn more about SI, click on the following links to the side. Below you will find previous SI worksheets, answer keys, and solutions videos for each week. In addition, for those of you who are taking SI with me currently, please fill out the weekly survey posted at the end of every study tables.
